Background: The university of Texas operates with a homegrown system and a highly customized stack of in-house tools and frameworks.

The project: Adding a modernized, Python/Django front end to the legacy mainframe course inventory system.

The legacy system: The back end is a mainframe coded with Natural/ADABAS, a procedural language similar to COBOL. Up until 2016, the only way for registrar employees to view the course inventory was using a maiframe interface. Coding the programs that ran each of the mainframe interfaces was a skill of its own. The legacy system is pictured.

***** pic of mainframe crin *****

The new system: A familiar interface designed with Python and Django, greatly expanding the functionality available to users, including a variety of search parameters for the course listing, a comparison function to view and compare semester data for a single course, and a change log. The back end remained on the mainframe as the registrar's data needed to remain accessible to all other departments while systems were being moved off of the mainframe campus-wide. The change log required a new ADABAS file, which underwent a design and approval process before its creation.
